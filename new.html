Education

Relocating to the UAE brings unique educational challenges, especially when adjusting to different curricula, teaching methods, and classroom dynamics. Public schools are Arabic-based and follow the UAE national curriculum, which can be an obstacle for non-Arabic speakers. In contrast, private schools offer a range of international options, including British, American, and IB programs, but these often vary significantly from the systems students may be used to, which can affect both learning styles and academic expectations. To ease the transition, it’s helpful to thoroughly research schools before arriving to find the curriculum and teaching approach that best suits your child’s needs. Many private schools also provide language support for non-native English and Arabic speakers, which can make adapting smoother. Joining expat or parent communities offers valuable insights and support, while being open to learning about UAE customs and values enriches the experience. These steps can help families build a positive, rewarding educational journey in the UAE.
